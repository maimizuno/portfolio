A website of digital agency

Background

This is a website of a theoretical web agency named “Sample”. Sample is opening up a business in Melbourne and going to offer various types of digital solutions such as website and software development services, social page creation and ads management at a reasonable cost.

The CEO, Satoshi Nakamoto, was running a similar business in Japan and had a business website, however, the website content was written in Japanese and needed to be translated into English.

Additionally, it hadn’t been updated since 2017 as the team were so busy working with their clients and also needed help with their own website overhaul to reflect the current situation.

Objectives

Sample’s focus initially was to rewrite the content in English, resolve the primary issues of their previous website and improve the appearance.

The first impression of their old website was rigidly formal and had a serious tone and a small amount of information. Although the content was neatly organised, the overall feeling no longer met the business objectives and needed rejuvenation.

The new target market of Sample would be small or start up businesses in Australia, who are seeking affordable digital solutions and growth opportunities for their businesses. In contrast with Japanese business persons, I thought that Australian audiences would prefer more friendly tone of the content and larger volume of information providing clarity of company’s focus and their offerings.

In conclusion, the key objective of rebuilding this website was to capture the new audience’s attention by providing the right amount of content that is unambiguous, visually attractive and has a feeling of familiarity.

Design Process

The general impression of Sample should be of an energetic start-up company offering futuristic ideas and technological solutions helping connect their clients with their customers more effectively.

String the right balance between enough information and not disengaging the readers is vitally important. As such, I needed to focus on the volume of content for the home page that allows users to instantly comprehend and maximises their attention. Using research studies it was recommended that up to 10 words for the title and 20 words for the sub title would be ideal. Keeping these guidelines in mind, I adopted some of these principles to the content writing.

Next, using these ideas I collected relevant pictures and a short video and created a logo that help users visualise the brand image and the services offered. Organising this information in a structured way improves and enhances the users’ experience through the flow and connection of key information. Specifically this was achieved by designing a unique layout that flows vertically and uses clearer links to capture readers’ eye. The navigation of the content’s motion and direction was also improved by utilising bigger margins and animation helping reduce distractions and busyness for the reader, creating a more enjoyable experience.

Finally but not least, I created the company’s logo and colour chart that represents the brand image, selected fonts that brings an additional value, and edited the video and pictures to make adjustment to the overall style of the website. I believe all of these tools make the website look more meaningful and engaging.

Colours

The colour scheme of this website was inspired by the video used on the cover page. I felt the combination of colours and gradients could give users great positive impressions like “high tech” and “futuristic” notions and cutting edge solutions.

I created multiple gradation patterns for different pages (services, news, about, contact) and for the link buttons adjust to the background colours, giving slight changes to each section and keeping the content readable.

Fonts

Equally important were the font selections in designing the website, these bring additional emotions and value to the content.

Searching for the best highly readable fonts, modern in style and that also fitted the brand’s image was the challenge. In the end I selected 2 fonts, “Arboria” and “Futura PT” that achieved the right result.

I felt that the geometric shape of these fonts best matched the cover video and also made the letters friendlier and warmer.
